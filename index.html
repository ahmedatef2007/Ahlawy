about her

Umm Kulthum is considered one of the greatest Arab musicians who ever lived. For over fifty years, she stunned the Arab world with her unmatchable voice; her vocals able to penetrate people’s hearts and express the soul of nations. Her lyrics were written by poets who eagerly offered their pieces to her in hopes of becoming the next esteemed songwriter ordained into Umm Kulthum’s repertoire. Beginning in 1934 and for almost forty years, Umm Kulthum performed monthly concerts in Cairo, Egypt (Danielson, 1). These concerts became the highlight of Arab life, as they were broadcast live on the radio, stretching far beyond the coasts of Egypt. Her renown exploded as households all across the Middle East considered these broadcasts as almost sacred, gathering with loved ones, making memories to the sound of her voice. Not only was she a musician, but an ambassador of the Arab world as she represented herself and her country throughout her life. She served as president of the Musicians’ Union, as a committee member on the government division of the arts, and as an Egyptian delegate to other Arab countries (28). She set the stage for Arab musicians who gained recognition after her. She is considered to be the most authentic producer of Egyptian and Arab music, recording some three hundred songs and creating a style and form that embodied the country’s musical tradition. She transformed the cultural perception of musical performance, taking it from an elite, restricted practice to a cultural norm, opening her concerts to the general public.

Important Events in her life

Umm Kulthum tours for Egypt 1967 - 1971

    After the Six-Day war defeat, she travelled throughout Egypt and the Arab world, donating the proceeds of her performances to the government of Egypt. These concerts were much publicized and took on the character of state visits

Umm Kulthum is knighted - 1944s

    King Farouk grants Umm Kulthum with the highest Egyptian order of Knighthood. The Egyptian order of Knighthood (Nishan Al-Kamal) had previously been reserved for royalty and politicians.

Umm Kulthum partners with Abdel Wahab

    Umm Kulthum partners with Mohammed Abdel Wahab 1965 As a contemporary of Umm Kulthum, Abdel Wahab was a highly regarded singer and songwriter who composed thousands of songs, his most famous collaboration with her was his composition Inta Omri

World of Umm Kulthum

    • Egypt | 1898-1975
    Umm Kulthum performaces across the Arab world and beyond spanned over fifty years, from when she sang with her father at special occasions in the Nile Delta until her final illness in Cairo.
    Tunisia | May 30-June9,1968
    On the one-year anniversary of the Six-Day war, Umm Kulthum shared a prayer ritual with first lady of Tunisia. The emotional photos were shown throughout the Arab world, demonstrating solidarity between Tunisia and Egypt.
    Morocco | March 1- 21, 1968
    To highlight the common faith uniting Arabs,she celebrated Eid Al Adha, an important Islamic holiday, with a needy Moroccan family. Moroccan citizens her show of shared faith by presenting her with a green candle representing Moses staff (walking stick).
    Libya | March 10-22, 1969
    In 1969, Muammar al-Gaddafi and his friends were ready to stage a coup of the Libyan government and set the date for March 12th. However, it turned out that Umm Kulthum was scheduled to perform that night. Out of respect for her and her songs dedicated to the Arab cause, Gaddafi postponed the revolution until she left Libya.
    Palestine 1945
    Umm Kulthum appeared in concert in Palestine in 1945. She later performed two benefit concerts in support of Fatah, to help the political group attain a leadership role in the Palestine Liberation Organization (PLO). The PLO was established to represent the Palestinian people.
    France (Paris) | November 9-22, 1967
    Umm Kulthum performs for the first time outside the Arab World in Paris in 1967
